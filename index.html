Air Purity:-94 AQI US

Air purity refers to the quality or cleanliness of the air, indicating the absence of pollutants, contaminants, or harmful substances. Pure air typically consists of a balanced mix of nitrogen (about 78%), oxygen (about 21%), and trace gases like argon, carbon dioxide, and water vapor. Air purity is often measured in terms of the levels of pollutants such as particulate matter (PM2.5 and PM10), carbon monoxide (CO), sulfur dioxide (SO₂), nitrogen oxides (NOx), ozone (O₃), volatile organic compounds (VOCs), and other harmful gases. High air purity means the air has low concentrations of these pollutants, making it safer and healthier to breathe.

Humidity:-68%

Humidity refers to the amount of water vapor present in the air. It is a key factor in weather and climate, affecting how hot or cold we feel. There are three main types of humidity:

  • Absolute Humidity: The total mass of water vapor in a given volume of air, usually measured in grams of water per cubic meter of air.
  • Relative Humidity: The percentage of water vapor present in the air compared to the maximum amount the air can hold at a specific temperature. Higher relative humidity makes the air feel warmer than it actually is.
  • Specific Humidity: The ratio of the mass of water vapor to the total mass of air (including the water vapor).

Humidity affects everything from weather patterns to human comfort, with high humidity making it feel hotter, and low humidity making it feel cooler.

What is COP28?

COP28 is the 28th session of the Conference of the Parties to the UN Framework Convention on Climate Change (UNFCCC). It was held in Dubai, UAE, in 2023, bringing together representatives from 197 countries to advance global climate action and meet the goals of the Paris Agreement.

Themes and Objectives

COP28 focused on critical areas including the Global Stocktake, energy transition, climate finance, and adaptation measures. It aimed to ensure global cooperation in reducing greenhouse gas emissions and building climate resilience.

Key Outcomes

  • Finalization of the Global Stocktake to assess progress under the Paris Agreement.
  • Operationalization of the Loss and Damage Fund to support vulnerable nations.
  • Commitments to triple renewable energy capacity and double energy efficiency improvements by 2030.
  • Climate finance goal set at $500 billion by 2025.

Challenges and Controversies

While COP28 saw progress, critics highlighted concerns over the inclusion of fossil fuel executives in leadership roles and gaps in funding for adaptation measures. The challenge remains to translate commitments into tangible actions.
